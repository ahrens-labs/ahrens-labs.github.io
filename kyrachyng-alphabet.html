Kyrachyng Alphabet

Learn the letters and sounds of the Kyrachyng language

About the Kyrachyng Alphabet: Kyrachyng is a constructed language with a rich phonetic system. This guide covers the basic consonants, vowels, and diphthongs used in the language, along with their IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) representations.

Consonants

Ch

[tʃ]

Like the "ch" in "church". Used frequently at the beginning of words and prefixes.

D

[d]

Like the "d" in "day". A voiced alveolar stop.

G

[g]

Like the "g" in "go". A voiced velar stop.

K

[k]

Like the "k" in "king". An unvoiced velar stop.

N

[n]

Like the "n" in "nose". A voiced alveolar nasal.

Ng

[ŋ]

Like the "ng" in "sing". A voiced velar nasal, often found at the end of words.

P

[p]

Like the "p" in "pat". An unvoiced bilabial stop.

R

[r]

Like the "r" in "run". Used in many Kyrachyng words.

T

[t]

Like the "t" in "top". An unvoiced alveolar stop.

Zh

[ʒ]

Like the "s" in "measure". A voiced postalveolar fricative.

Z

[z]

Like the "z" in "zero". A voiced alveolar fricative.

B

[b]

Like the "b" in "boy". A voiced bilabial stop.

Vowels

A

[æ]

Like the "a" in "cat". An unrounded front vowel.

E

[ɛ]

Like the "e" in "bed". An open-mid front unrounded vowel.

I

[i]

Like the "i" in "fleece". A close front unrounded vowel.

O

[oʊ]

Like the "o" in "go". A close-mid back rounded vowel.

U

[u]

Like the "u" in "goose". A close back rounded vowel.

Oi

[ɔɪ]

Like the "oy" in "boy". A diphthong combining open back and close front vowels.

Diphthongs

Ai

[aɪ]

Like the "i" in "five". Combines "a" and "i" sounds.

Ao

[aʊ]

Like the "ou" in "out". Combines "a" and "o" sounds.

Oa

[oʊə]

A gliding vowel, like "o" sound shifting toward "a".

Consonant Clusters

Tch

[tʃ]

An affricate sound, used in some word variations.

Zh

[ʒ]

A voiced postalveolar fricative, appearing frequently in Kyrachyng words.

Gh

[x] or [ɣ]

A fricative sound, used in specific contexts within the language.

Common Phonetic Patterns

Word Endings: -ch

[tʃ]

Many nouns and verbs in Kyrachyng end with the "ch" sound, particularly for pronouns and conjugated verbs. Examples: daCH (I), zhroCH (he/she)

Word Endings: -ing

[ɪŋ]

Common ending for descriptive words and some noun formations. Examples: zakyng (sun), calorming

Word Endings: -an

[æn]

Frequently used in nouns and objects. Examples: grizetan (chair), rapotang (park)

Prefix: Ch-

[tʃ]

Used as a prefix for first-person singular verb conjugations. Examples: Chococanoy (I am), Chotiroy (I run)

Prefix: Bi-

[bi]

Used to indicate past tense in verbs. Examples: Bicocanoy (I was), Bitiroy (I ran)

Prefix: Ov- or Ova-

[oʊv] or [oʊvə]

Indicates plurality or augmentation. Examples: Ova-drach (happy ones), ovantryn (best)

Writing System Notes

Capitalization

Plural nouns are typically capitalized. The first letter of sentences and proper nouns are capitalized, following English conventions.

Phonetic Precision

Kyrachyng aims for phonetic consistency. The written form closely represents pronunciation, making it relatively easy to pronounce once the phonetic rules are learned.

IPA Reference

The IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) representations provided help non-native speakers understand the exact articulation of each sound. Compare with familiar sounds in your native language.
